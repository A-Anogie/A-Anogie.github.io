    • Andrew Anogie Uduimoh

    Lecturer, Cybersecurity Science, Federal University of Technology Minna

    Sample: How can we create cybersecurity systems that are not only robust and adaptive but also profoundly attuned to the human experience? In a world where privacy and security are constantly under threat, designing solutions that balance technical sophistication with user engagement is critical. This challenge has driven my research and professional journey, focusing on Privacy and Security, Human-Centered Computing, Human Factors in Cybersecurity, and a recent interest in Artificial Intelligence (AI). My goal is to advance these fields by developing solutions that address and balance cybersecurity's technical and human aspects. Pursuing a Ph.D. at Clemson University in the Human-Centered Computing (HCC) program aligns perfectly with this career goal. My academic and professional background has provided a solid foundation for my research. My Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and Computer Science offered essential insights into computing principles, while my Master’s in Cybersecurity deepened my understanding of security challenges. As a lecturer in the Cyber Security Department at the Federal University of Technology Minna for the past six years, I have taught a range of courses, including ethical hacking, digital forensics, and AI. This position has also given me the opportunity to supervise more than 16 undergraduate research projects, which has expanded my expertise and fueled my passion for developing human-centered security solutions.
