    • Andrew Anogie Uduimoh

    Lecturer, Cybersecurity Science, Federal University of Technology Minna

    I am a Lecturer in the Cyber Security Science Department at the Federal University of Technology, Minna, where I teach and guide research on human-centered security solutions. My work focuses on critical areas like Privacy and Security, Digital Forensics, and Human Factors in Cybersecurity, alongside a recent emphasis on Artificial Intelligence. My research aims to enhance cybersecurity awareness and develop culturally relevant, user-centered training tools, particularly for Nigerian contexts. I have led impactful projects, such as ScamNada, a dynamic anti-scam framework for digital banking, and a ransomware awareness game, both designed to improve user resilience to cyber threats. My research has been published in international journals and conferences, with applications spanning mobile banking, digital forensics, and the security of critical infrastructures in Nigeria. I am committed to advancing cybersecurity knowledge and education in ways that bridge technical expertise with real-world user needs.
